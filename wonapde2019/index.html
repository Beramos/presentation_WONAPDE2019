Summary

Simplified model (LNP):

  • Steady-state & transient
  • Efficient
  • Only underlimiting

Poisson-Nernst-Planck (PNP):

  • Transient
  • Computationally demanding
  • Underlimiting & overlimiting

→ Need for efficient overlimiting model

  • Singularly-perturbed PDE
  • Very fine mesh resolution (Âµm)
  • Small timesteps
  • Fouling is a slow transient process

Reynolds-averaging

$${\displaystyle {\phi(x,t) = \overline{\phi(x)} + \phi(x,t)' }}$$

RANS for magnetohydrodynamics:

  • Wilson, Craft, Iacovides (2014)
  • ...

RANS for electroconvection:

  • Kourmatzis, Shrimpton (2018)

→ Limited research

The paper on magnetohydrodynamics did only consider an imposed magnetic fields and considers a homogeneous electrically conducting fluid. This is a simpler approach

The transport of charged species is done through a single equation, aka the space charge transport equation for which the boundary conditions are known, because the charge is injected in the system. In our case the charge emerges from the simulation. There are more equations this way. There is also no bulk convective flow. Future work is recommended in the modelling of chaotic and turbulent EHD flows that have a bulk convective flow.

Conclusion

Challenges:

  • Electrodialysis at overlimiting currents (mass transfer limitations)
  • Extended Poisson-Nernst-Planck

Opportunities:

  • Electroneutrality assumption (no mass transfer limitations)
  • Reynolds-averaging
    • average turbulent fluctuation = 0
    • 2-3 new terms to be modelled
  • Not only for electrodialysis
